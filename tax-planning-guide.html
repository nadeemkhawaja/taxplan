Step 5: Advanced Tax Strategies for Multi-Income Filers

🎯 Objective: These strategies leverage the unique opportunities of having W-2 income + self-employment + investment income to legally minimize your tax burden.

Strategy 1: QBI Deduction (Qualified Business Income)

HIGH PRIORITY

20% Deduction on Pass-Through Business Income

If you have self-employment income, you may qualify for a 20% deduction on your qualified business income (QBI), subject to limitations based on W-2 wages and property basis.

Strategy 2: Capital Loss Harvesting

HIGH PRIORITY

Deploy Carryforward Losses Strategically

If you have capital loss carryforwards from prior years (e.g., from a failed investment or business liquidation), you can use up to $3,000/year against ordinary income. Excess carries forward indefinitely.

Strategy 3: Dependent Employment & Payroll Planning

MEDIUM PRIORITY

Employ Family Members in Your Business

If you employ dependents (teenagers, college students) through your business, you can deduct their wages and they benefit from low tax brackets. Must document real work and issue proper W-2s or 1099-NEC.

Requirements:
  • Must be a legitimate employee under dependency test
  • Work must be real and documented
  • Wages must be reasonable for the work
  • Must issue W-2 if over $600; otherwise 1099-NEC

Strategy 4: Mileage Tracking & Business Use of Home

HIGH PRIORITY

Maximize Deductible Business Expenses

Mileage: Track business miles (client visits, supplies, etc.) at the IRS standard rate (~0.70/mile for 2024). Home Office: If you use a dedicated room/space, deduct a portion of rent/mortgage, utilities, insurance.

Tracking Tools: MileIQ, Everlance, or manual logs. Start TODAY — prior years cannot be recovered without contemporaneous records.

Strategy 5: 1099-NEC Filing & Contractor Classification

HIGH PRIORITY

Proper Tax Reporting for Contractors & Vendors

If you pay domestic contractors >$600/year, file Form 1099-NEC. Filing deadlines: January 31 to recipients, January 31 to IRS (or Feb 28 if filing electronically).

⚠️ Compliance Note: Failure to file 1099-NEC can result in penalties ($50–$270+ per form). Use Tax1099.com or your CPA's filing service for late filings.

Strategy 6: Health Insurance Premium Deduction (Self-Employed)

MEDIUM PRIORITY

Above-the-Line Deduction for Health Insurance

Caveat: If you're eligible for employer-sponsored health insurance through your W-2 job, you CANNOT deduct premiums for self-funded plans. However, if no employer plan is available, you can deduct 100% of premiums you pay for yourself, spouse, and dependents.
